Friday 24th April is the Feast Day of St Mark. We remember St Mark as the author/collator of the Gospel which bears
his name, probably the first of the four Gospels to be set down in the form in which we have them. Mark joined St
Paul and St Barnabas on their first missionary journey and later spent time with St Paul and St Peter in Rome. His
Gospel is based on the teaching and memoirs of St Peter. St Mark is thought to have established the Church in
Alexandria, and to have died as a martyr there.

Thursday 23rd is the Feast Day of St George, thought to be a soldier living in Palestine at the beginning of the fourth
century. He was martyred at Lydda in 304, the beginning of the Diocletian persecution, and widely revered throughout
the Christian East. He replaced Edward the Confessor as the patron saint of England following the Crusades, and
Edward III made him patron of the Order of the Garter.
