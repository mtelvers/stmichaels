Next Sunday 10th May, we have our annual church meetings after our 10.30am Mass. As well as electing our
Churchwardens, PCC and representatives to Deanery Synod, this is also a time to receive the annual accounts for
2025, to receive the PCC’s Annual report, as well as reports on the church buildings, bellringers and other groups –
and to ask any questions you might have about the work of the PCC or the mission and ministry of St Michael’s
church.

If you would like to seek appointment as a Churchwarden, member of the PCC, or as one of our representatives
on the Deanery Synod please complete a nomination form, available at Back of Church or from Eleanor. Make sure
your form is signed by a proposer and seconder, and then pop the nomination form into the box at Back of Church.

To be elected to the PCC, you must be aged 16 or over; an actual communicant member of the church, and (if you are
aged 18 or over) your name must have been on the Electoral Roll for at least six months. To be elected Churchwarden
you must be aged 21 or over, an actual communicant member, and your name must be on the church Electoral Roll.

We are delighted by the appointment of Bishop Martin Warner to be the next Vicar of St Peter’s Folkestone. He
will take up this post following his retirement as Bishop of Chichester at the end of this month, and we look forward
to his faith, prayers and energy in the Society parishes of Canterbury diocese.
